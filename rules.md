# Canglish Phonetic Rules

Welcome to Canglish! This guide will help you understand how to pronounce Cantonese words using English phonetics.

### Special Consonant Combinations

- **mmm** - A prolonged "m" sound, like humming with lips closed (mmm = not)
- **nn** - Nasal "n" sound
- **ng** - Nasal sound from the back of throat, like in "sing" but can start a word

## Compound Words

Many Cantonese words are formed by combining two or more syllables:

- Use a space or hyphen to separate syllables: `ma-ma`, `pang-yau`, `lou-baan`
- Some compounds use spaces: `mean bao` (bread), `oiw yuk` (beef)
- Colors add `sik` (color): `hong sik` (red), `lam sik` (blue)
- Days of week use `sing kay` + number: `sing kay yut` (Monday)

## Sentence Structure Basics

### Word Order

Cantonese generally follows: **Subject + Verb + Object**

Examples:

- `ore sik mean bao` = I eat bread
- `lay yum cha` = You drink tea

### Negation

Place `mmm` (not) or `mou` (don't have/lacking) before the verb:

- `mmm ho` = not good
- `mou chin` = don't have money

### Questions

Add `ma` at the end of a statement to make it a question:

- `lay ho ma?` = Are you good? / How are you?
- `lay sik ma?` = Do you eat?

Alternative: Use question words like `mat yea` (what), `been dee` (where), `gay` (how many)

### Softening Statements

Add `aaa` at the end to soften the tone:

- `ho aaa` = good (softer, friendlier)

## Common Grammar Patterns

### Possession

Use the particle after the possessor (not shown in basic phonetics, but context helps):

- Family terms often indicate relationship: `ore ma-ma` (my mum)

### Plurals

Repetition can indicate plural or emphasis:

- `yut yut` = every day
- `mui mui` = younger sister (repeated for the name)

### Comparisons

- `tai` + adjective = too (adjective): `tai gwai` (too expensive)
- adjective + `dee` = more (adjective): `ping dee` (cheaper)

### Politeness

- `mmm goi` = please / thank you (for service)
- Use at the beginning or end of requests

## Numbers and Counting

### Basic Numbers (1-10)

- yut (1), yee (2), saam (3), say (4), nnnnng (5)
- lok (6), chat (7), bat (8), gow (9), sup (10)

### Compound Numbers

- 11-19: sup + number (e.g., `sup yut` = 11)
- 20, 30, etc.: number + sup (e.g., `yee sup` = 20, `saam sup` = 30)
- Large numbers: `yut bak` (100), `yut chin` (1000)

## Tone Tips

**Important Note:** Cantonese is a tonal language with 6-9 tones depending on classification. This phonetic system doesn't indicate tones, which means:

1. **Context is key** - The same phonetic spelling can mean different things (e.g., `mai` = buy OR sell)
2. **Practice with native speakers** - Listen to how words sound in context
3. **Don't worry too much** - Context usually makes meaning clear
4. **Start with common phrases** - Build familiarity through repetition

## Common Patterns to Remember

### Same Sound, Different Meaning

Some words sound identical but mean different things based on context:

- `mai` = buy OR sell
- `hai` = yes OR shoes OR crab
- `ma` = question marker OR horse OR mum (ma-ma)

### Helpful Particles

- `ma` - Makes a question
- `aaa` - Softens a statement
- `mmm` - Negates (not)
- `mmm goi` - Please/thank you

### Family Terms

Use reduplication (repeating syllables):

- `ma-ma` (mum), `ba-ba` (dad)
- `gaw gaw` (older brother), `jee jee` (older sister)
- `po po` (grandmother), `yea yea` (grandfather)

## Learning Tips

1. **Start with greetings and basics** - `hi`, `joy ghin` (goodbye), `mmm goi` (please)
2. **Learn question words early** - `mat yea` (what), `been dee` (where), `gay` (how many)
3. **Practice pronouns** - `ore` (I), `lay` (you), `koy` (he/she)
4. **Build from simple to complex** - Master single syllables before compound words
5. **Use it in context** - Practice phrases, not just individual words
6. **Listen actively** - The more you hear, the better you'll understand tone patterns
7. **Don't fear mistakes** - Native speakers appreciate the effort!

## Quick Reference: Essential Phrases

- `ore gew ___` = My name is \_\_\_
- `lay gew mat yea?` = What's your name?
- `mmm goi` = Please/Thank you
- `ho` = Good
- `mmm ho` = Not good
- `ore chung-ee ___` = I like \_\_\_
- `ore oi ___` = I want \_\_\_
- `gay dor chin?` = How much money?
- `been dee?` = Where?
- `mat yea?` = What?
